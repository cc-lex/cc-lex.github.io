Take Action

Find out what you can do to help stop climate change.


  • Spread the Word

    • Talk to family and friends.
    • Send messages on social media.

  • Renewable Energy

    • Using renewable energy as the power source for your home is sometimes cheaper than using non-renewable energy!
    • Renewable energy produces no greenhouse gases, which is good for the environment.

  • Improve Your Home

    • Many houses in America use more heating or cooling than they need to.
    • Most of the time this is due to insulation problems or heat/cold air esacping through holes.
    • But sometimes the system itself is the problem. If you have an old system, it may be too inefficient and use up a lot of energy. You can solve this by buying upgrading to a newer system.

  • Save Water

    • Take shorter showers.
    • Use a dishwasher.
    • Don't leave the tap running when you're brushing your teeth.

  • Lightbulbs

    • An older, incandescent bulb can uses significant amount compared to an LED.
    • You actually save money by buying newer bulbs because the amount of energy saved would make you the money back.

  • Power Eaters

    • "Because the appliance is off means it isn't drawing any power." Well, most of the time, an appliance can still draw power even when shut off. So the smart thing to do would be to unplug those appliances instead of turning them off.
    • For example, if you left a laptop computer plugged in, even when fully charged, it will use 4.5 kWH (kilowatt hours) of electricity in a week!

  • Fuel Efficient

    • Hybrid cars and fully electric vehicles save gas due to their design.
    • Hybrid cars have an average fuel economy of 54.4 MPG (miles per gallon), while gas cars have an average fuel economy of 24.9 MPG.

  • Don't Take the Car

    • Gas cars and hybrid cars release greenhouse gases into the air, which is harmful to the cliamte. Even electrical cars are mainly still gas powered, because most of the time that energy used to charge the car was produced by a non-renewable source.
    • Ride a bike, or walk instead.
